Hamilton in Chicago was quite the concert!

Am I right ... ?

And the "Hamilton" actor for Lafayette did not throw away his shot.

Here's an example of one of his most difficult verses:

And I’m never gonna stop until I make ‘em / Drop and burn ‘em up and scatter their remains

What makes it difficult is that it's one of the fastest line ever spoken on Broadway, according to analysis form FiveThirtyEight.

And just how fast?

6.3 words per second

Belmont student Chris Lee stepped into the big shoes of Daveed Diggs, a professional rapper, to the thunderous applause of the Chicago audience when he rapped that fast line. Major props to Chicago's Lafayette character!

Here's how the line's speed compares to other musicals that have all come to Chicago, according to FiveThirtyEight:

Musical Song Words Length Speed (Words/Sec.)
Pirates of Penzance Modern Major-General 34 9 3.8
Hamilton Satisfied 121 24 5.0
Company Not Getting Married Today 68 11 6.2
Hamilton Guns and Ships 19 3 6.3

While Lee's verse in "Guns and Ships" isn't the fastest in history, I couldn't believe it is delivered in one breathe. That's what seems like what made it so unique.
